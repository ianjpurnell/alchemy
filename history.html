History:

Alchemy first arose in hellenistic Egypt. It combined elements of different sciences such as metallurgy and pharmacy with philosophy. Over the centuries it was outlawed in many countries. Its practice of turning things to gold ( chrysopoeia) was considered vulgar, deceitful, greedy, and in some cases, blasphemous. As chemistry became more modern and more and more was discovered about the nature of matter, chemists began to look down on the older practices and theories, despite them being based somewhat in science. We could not have gotten to where we are today without alchemy.
